Description: Parkinson's disease is a progressive nervous system disorder that affects movement, causing tremors, stiffness, and difficulty with balance and coordination.

Symptoms: Early symptoms include tremors, slow movement (bradykinesia), stiffness, and difficulty with balance and posture. Non-motor symptoms can include depression, sleep disorders, and cognitive impairment.

Behavior: Over time, patients may have trouble walking, talking, and completing simple tasks.

Causes: The exact cause is unknown, but it involves the gradual breakdown of dopamine-producing neurons in the brain. Genetic and environmental factors may contribute.

Prevention: There is no known way to prevent Parkinson’s disease, but regular physical exercise and a healthy diet can help manage symptoms and slow progression.

Precautions: Early detection is key. If any symptoms appear, consult a healthcare provider. Support groups, therapy, and medication can assist in managing the disease.

Tremors: Often starting in the hands or fingers, especially at rest.

Bradykinesia: Slowness of movement, making everyday tasks challenging.

Muscle Rigidity: Stiffness in the muscles that can limit the range of motion.

Postural Instability: Issues with balance and coordination, leading to a higher risk of falls.

Non-Motor Symptoms:

Cognitive Impairment: Although not as pronounced as in Alzheimer's, cognitive decline can occur, affecting executive functions and memory.
