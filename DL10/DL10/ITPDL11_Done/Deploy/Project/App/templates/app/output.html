Mild Dementia

Mild Dementia is an early stage where cognitive decline becomes noticeable, but individuals can still perform most daily tasks independently.

Symptoms:

  • Memory lapses, especially for recent events.
  • Difficulty planning or organizing.
  • Losing or misplacing items.
  • Struggling to find words or names.

Causes:

  • Age-related brain changes.
  • Alzheimer’s disease or vascular conditions.

Prevention:

  • Stay mentally active (puzzles, learning new skills).
  • Regular physical exercise.
  • Maintain social connections.

Precautions:

  • Routine health check-ups.
  • Avoid isolation.
  • Engage in memory exercises.

Possible Affected Disease:

  • Alzheimer's Disease.

Food Plan:

  • Mediterranean diet rich in vegetables, whole grains, fish.
  • Avoid processed foods and sugars.

Vitamin Body Levels:

  • Ensure adequate intake of Vitamin E, B12, and Omega-3 fatty acids.
{% elif predict == 'ModerateDemented' %}

Moderate Dementia

Moderate Dementia involves significant memory loss and confusion that may interfere with daily life.

Symptoms:

  • Confusion about where they are or what day it is.
  • Increased memory loss and forgetfulness.
  • Difficulty performing basic tasks (dressing, bathing).

Causes:

  • Alzheimer’s or other neurodegenerative conditions.
  • Brain trauma or stroke.

Prevention:

  • Continue engaging in mental and physical activities.
  • Control risk factors (blood pressure, diabetes).

Precautions:

  • Regular health screening.
  • Monitor diet and ensure safe environments.

Possible Affected Disease:

  • Alzheimer's Disease, Vascular Dementia.

Food Plan:

  • Balanced meals with lean protein, whole grains, and leafy greens.

Vitamin Body Levels:

  • Focus on Vitamin D, Omega-3s, and antioxidants.

Non-Parkinson

This classification indicates an individual who does not have Parkinson's Disease but may have similar motor symptoms.

Symptoms:

  • Tremors, slow movements.
  • Stiffness and balance issues.

Causes:

  • Secondary causes like drug side effects or other neurodegenerative diseases.

Prevention:

  • Regular physical activity.
  • Early treatment of any underlying conditions.

Precautions:

  • Avoid medications that affect the nervous system.
  • Regular neurological check-ups.

Possible Affected Disease:

  • Essential Tremor, Multiple System Atrophy (MSA).

Food Plan:

  • High-antioxidant foods, such as berries, and Omega-3 rich foods.

Vitamin Body Levels:

  • Adequate intake of Vitamin D and CoQ10.

Parkinson’s Disease

Parkinson's Disease is a neurodegenerative disorder affecting movement and motor function.

Symptoms:

  • Tremors (shaking), slow movement, stiffness.
  • Impaired balance and coordination.
  • Muscle rigidity and tremors at rest.

Causes:

  • Genetic and environmental factors.
  • Loss of dopamine-producing neurons in the brain.

Prevention:

  • Early detection of symptoms.
  • Healthy lifestyle with physical activity.

Precautions:

  • Monitor medication that can impact motor function.
  • Regular medical evaluations.

Possible Affected Disease:

  • Multiple System Atrophy, Lewy Body Dementia.

Food Plan:

  • Focus on fiber-rich foods for digestive health.
  • Omega-3 fatty acids, nuts, and seeds.

Vitamin Body Levels:

  • Ensure sufficient Vitamin D, B-complex, and magnesium.

Very Mild Dementia

Very Mild Dementia shows early signs of cognitive impairment, which may not significantly impact daily life.

Symptoms:

  • Occasional memory lapses.
  • Difficulty in complex problem-solving.

Causes:

  • Early stages of Alzheimer's or vascular-related issues.

Prevention:

  • Brain-stimulating activities.
  • Regular social and physical activities.

Precautions:

  • Early intervention in cognitive exercises.
  • Regular health screening.

Possible Affected Disease:

  • Alzheimer's Disease, Vascular Dementia.

Food Plan:

  • Plant-based diet with high levels of antioxidants.

Vitamin Body Levels:

  • Focus on Vitamin E, B-complex vitamins, and Omega-3s.
{% elif predict == 'Alzheimer' %}

Alzheimer’s Disease

Alzheimer’s Disease is a progressive brain disorder that slowly destroys memory and cognitive skills.

Symptoms:

  • Memory loss, difficulty with familiar tasks.
  • Mood and personality changes.

Causes:

  • Build-up of amyloid plaques and tau tangles in the brain.
  • Genetic factors.

Prevention:

  • Mental, physical, and social activities.

Precautions:

  • Early diagnosis and treatment.
  • Regular health check-ups.

Possible Affected Disease:

  • Vascular Dementia, Frontotemporal Dementia.

Food Plan:

  • Mediterranean diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins.

Vitamin Body Levels:

  • Focus on antioxidants, B vitamins, and Vitamin D.
