Brain Disorder
Stages

Neurodegenerative diseases progress through distinct stages, each with unique symptoms and care requirements. Understanding these stages helps clinicians intervene at the right time and patients prepare for what lies ahead.


Our AI classifies brain MRI scans into one of four stages below, giving clinicians an instant, data-backed second opinion.

No Impairment

No Impairment Stage

No noticeable symptoms are present. The individual performs all daily activities without difficulty and shows no measurable memory loss or cognitive decline. Brain structure appears normal on standard imaging, though microscopic changes may already be forming at a cellular level. Regular monitoring is still advised for those with a family history of neurodegenerative disease.

Normal cognition Full independence No treatment needed Annual checkups advised
Very Mild

Very Mild Impairment

Subtle but noticeable cognitive difficulties begin to emerge. The individual may experience occasional memory lapses — forgetting names or misplacing items — and slight challenges with complex problem-solving. These symptoms are often dismissed as normal ageing. The person remains fully independent and can manage all daily tasks without external help. Close monitoring and lifestyle adjustments (diet, exercise, mental stimulation) can slow progression.

Minor memory lapses Still fully independent Close monitoring needed Lifestyle changes beneficial
Mild

Mild Impairment Stage

Cognitive changes become noticeable to family and colleagues. Memory gaps are more frequent, concentration is reduced, and mood swings or anxiety may appear. The individual might get lost in familiar places or repeat questions. They can still live independently but may need occasional reminders for appointments or medications. Early medical intervention — including cognitive therapy and medication — can significantly slow the progression at this stage.

Noticeable memory loss Mood changes possible Mostly independent Medical intervention advised Cognitive therapy helps
Moderate

Moderate Impairment Stage

Significant and wide-ranging difficulties emerge across memory, reasoning, language, and daily tasks. The individual may struggle to recognise close family members, lose track of time and place, and require help with dressing, eating, or personal hygiene. Behavioural changes such as agitation, wandering, or depression are common. Full-time care or assisted living is often necessary. Treatment focuses on managing symptoms, maintaining quality of life, and supporting caregivers.

Severe memory loss Daily assistance required Behavioural changes Language difficulties Full-time care often needed

What is a CNN?

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are the backbone of modern image recognition. They automatically learn spatial hierarchies of features from images through multiple layers — detecting edges, shapes, and complex patterns. In NeuroDetect, CNNs analyse MRI scans to identify early disease markers.

What is an ANN?

Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) are machine learning models inspired by the human brain. Composed of interconnected layers of neurons, ANNs learn from labelled data and generalise to unseen examples — ideal for multi-class medical classification tasks like staging disease severity.
