Parkinson's Disease Presence Detected

Parkinson's disease is a progressive nervous system disorder affecting movement. Early consultation with a healthcare provider is strongly recommended.

Overview

Description & Causes

Parkinson's disease involves the gradual breakdown of dopamine-producing neurons in the brain. The exact cause is unknown, but genetic and environmental factors may contribute. There is no known way to prevent it, but early detection and management can significantly improve quality of life.

Motor Symptoms

Motor Symptoms

  • Tremors — Often starting in hands at rest
  • Bradykinesia — Slowness of movement
  • Muscle Rigidity — Stiffness limiting range of motion
  • Postural Instability — Balance and coordination issues
Non-Motor Symptoms

Non-Motor Symptoms

  • Cognitive Impairment — Executive function & memory
  • Mood Disorders — Depression, anxiety, apathy
  • Sleep Disturbances — REM sleep behaviour disorder
  • Autonomic Dysfunction — Constipation, urinary issues
Behavioural Changes

Behavioural Changes

  • Impulsivity — May relate to medication side effects
  • Speech Changes — Soft, slurred, or monotone
  • Masked Face — Reduced facial muscle activity
Management

Precautions & Management

  • Consult a neurologist immediately
  • Regular physical exercise to manage symptoms
  • Medication and cognitive therapy
  • Support groups and caregiver assistance
Comparative Analysis

Parkinson's vs Alzheimer's — Key Differences

  • Cognitive: AD shows more pronounced memory loss; PD has more executive dysfunction and attention problems
  • Emotional: AD more likely presents with hallucinations/paranoia; PD shows more depression and anxiety
  • Daily Functioning: AD declines rapidly in daily tasks; PD motor symptoms are progressive but cognitive symptoms more gradual
